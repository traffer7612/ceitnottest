Ceitnot

An autonomous, yield-aware credit engine on EVM networks. Users deposit yield-bearing ERC-4626 collateral, borrow a stablecoin (ceitUSD in CDP mode), and the protocol’s Yield Siphon routes realized collateral appreciation into a reduction of effective debt for the market — without iterating every user in one transaction, and without the frontend ever holding private keys.

1. Executive summary

Ceitnot is a lending protocol that treats yield from collateral as a first-class input to solvency. While traditional lending is mostly “you borrowed X, you owe interest until you repay”, Ceitnot adds a second axis: when the collateral vault’s share price increases (NAV / share in asset terms, via ERC-4626 convertToAssets), the protocol can apply that accrual against outstanding debt through a global scaling mechanism, executed by a permissionless or keeper-triggered harvestYield call.

That design is the Yield Siphon. It is not magic money printing: it is accounting that moves value from collateral revaluation and vault yield into a reduction in effective debt for all borrowers in a market, bounded by oracles, rounding rules, a heartbeat, and the reality that price can fall faster than yield accrues — which is where liquidations and risk parameters matter.

Where it lives: one engine proxy users and integrations call; a market registry maps each marketId to an ERC-4626 vault, an oracle, risk (LTV, liquidation threshold, penalty), and optional IRM. ceitUSD in CDP mode is minted when users borrow and burned on repay. A PSM (optional deployment) can stabilize the peg to external stables. Governance (token + ve + governor + timelock) is the intended path for parameter changes in production.

One stable engine address; each marketId pulls config (vault + oracle + risk) from the registry. PSM and ceitUSD sit beside the core credit loop.

2. The problem we solve

Many DeFi borrowers hold productive assets (LST, vault shares, compounding stables) but still service debt as if the collateral were static. The mental overhead is high: claim rewards, sell, swap, manually repay, repeat. Ceitnot’s Siphon is a protocol-native attempt to align principal reduction with the economic reality that collateral often earns on-chain yield, while keeping solvency enforcement explicit through health checks and liquidations.

We do not claim that yield always outruns borrow costs or mark-to-market losses. Interest still compounds on debt via the IRM. Price risk is still the dominant tail risk for volatile collaterals. Siphon is a convenience and capital-efficiency feature inside a conservative risk framework, not a promise of risk-free carry.

Siphon tries to encode part of that loop on-chain, not to remove prudence.

Three clocks: vault accrual, debt interest, mark price of the collateral asset in USD.

4. Glossary (plain English)

ERC-4626 (tokenized vault)A standard for “pool my tokens, give me a share”. The share’s value in the underlying can rise (yield, or NAV changes). Ceitnot collateral is held as vault shares, not “raw” token unless the vault is 1:1.
ceitUSD (CDP debt unit)When the engine mints to your address on borrow, you hold ceitUSD as liquidity; simultaneously your position records principal debt. Repay burns ceitUSD and shrinks what you owe.
LTV vs liquidation threshold (LT)LTV caps how much you can borrow per dollar of effective collateral. The liquidation threshold is higher (more conservative) so there remains buffer for penalties and liquidator incentives before the position is underwater in mark-to-market terms.
Health Factor (HF)A cross-asset scalar: HF ≥ 1 means your position is not eligible for standard liquidation. < 1 means liquidations may occur (subject to protocol rules).
globalDebtScale (RAY)Shared multiplier that reduces the effect of notional debt after yield is harvested — without looping over all users. Personal debt “settles” to the current scale on your next interaction.
PSM (Peg Stability Module)A swap between ceitUSD and an external stable with tight fees, used to manage peg pressure and on/off-ramps for the stable side of the system.
TimelockTime delay between a passed governance action and on-chain execution — public reaction window.

The diagram is not to scale — use live parameters from the registry for your deployment.

5. End-to-end user journey

Below is the chronological story of a position. Wording is simplified; gas, approvals, and exact function names are in the contracts / UI.

The steps below add narrative detail to this pipeline.

No private keys in the app server; at most an RPC and cached config.

Connect a wallet (MetaMask, Rabby, …). The web app is a view + transaction sender; it does not store seed phrases. Every state-changing call is signed by you.
Pick a market (marketId). The UI reads MarketRegistry for vault symbol, LTV, caps, and oracles. If the market is isolated, you cannot “use” that collateral to back borrowing on other markets in the same cross way.
Deposit ERC-4626 shares into the engine. Your position stores collateralShares. The system values collateral in USD for HF using the relay price and vault conversion.
Borrow (mint ceitUSD) up to the allowed LTV and market caps. You now have spendable ceitUSD and a recorded debt position. Interest begins to accrue per the IRM and utilization.
Time passes. Two things happen in parallel: (a) vault share price may increase from yield, (b) your debt grows from borrow interest. These are not the same — see sections 6–7.
Harvest (anyone, subject to heartbeat and min-yield): harvestYield(marketId) materializes the vault delta since the last snapshot into a debt credit to the market via globalDebtScale, minus an optional yieldFeeBps to reserves.
Repay or withdraw when you choose. If HF would fall too low, withdraw is limited. Repay burns ceitUSD and reduces debt.
Liquidation if HF < 1. A third party repays your debt in the debt token and seizes a portion of your collateral, including a liquidation penalty / spread that rewards the liquidator and (optionally) the protocol. Bad tail outcomes can become bad debt with reserve absorption.

You must think about both flows. Strong vault yield does not automatically mean “free loan” if borrow APR is high or HF is tight.

6. Yield Siphon (deep dive)

6.1 Intuition

Think of a single lever that scales everyone’s debt notional in a market, instead of trying to add payments to each borrower separately. When the vault’s assets per share has risen since the last harvest, the system converts that incremental asset amount into an equivalent debt token amount using the oracle, then shrinks the effective total debt by moving the global scale. Your personal row in storage still has a “principal” field, but the current debt you owe is principal times the effective scale — so when the scale drops, the effective amount you owe drops for everyone proportionally.

The key idea: one global update propagates to all positions the next time each borrower touches the system (or their view is materialized in the UI with the same rules).

6.2 Why it is O(1)

Updating one globalDebtScale and a few market snapshot fields (last price per share, last harvest time) is constant cost in the number of users. This matters on L2: you avoid unbounded loops over positions that would make harvest unusable at scale.

Lazy settlement shifts per-user work from harvest to interact / liquidate / view, keeping the shared path cheap.

6.3 Lazy settlement

When you interact later, the engine settles your position to the new scale. Until then, your on-disk “principal” may look stale; the true debt is the compositional view. This is standard index accounting (like interest indices in classic lending) but applied to realized yield impact on the collateral side.

6.4 Guardrails in plain language

  • Heartbeat — you cannot harvest every block; the protocol enforces a minimum interval to reduce noise and gameability.
  • Min harvest size (if configured) — avoid dust harvest spam.
  • Rounding — debt is rounded up in favor of the protocol where applicable; the scale after yield is conservative so the protocol does not “over-forgive” debt by accident.
  • Oracle failure — if price is zero or bad, the harvest path typically aborts the debt credit for safety.
Critical nuance. Siphon helps when the collateral has positive carry or re-prices up. If collateral market price crashes, Siphon is not a substitute for liquidation. The protocol’s first line of defense against insolvency in a downturn is risk parameters + liquidations, not the hope that “yield will catch up”.

7. Interest rates (IRM) — separate from Siphon

The Interest Rate Model is a classic kinked curve: a base rate plus a first slope below a utilization kink, and a steeper second slope above the kink, because borrowing becomes scarcer. Part of the interest is routed to the protocol via a reserve factor (a slice of accrual that builds totalReserves to absorb bad debt and fund maintenance).

Why this matters: Siphon reduces effective debt from collateral yield, while the IRM charges you for the privilege of holding debt as a function of pool utilization. In stressed regimes, IRM can spike — you can still be worse off on net even with positive vault yield if the borrow side is expensive and your position is small / short-lived. Always look at net carry and HF margin, not headline APY in isolation.

Siphon does not replace this curve. It is a collateral-side term; the IRM is the debt / pool utilization term.

8. Health factor & solvency

At a high level, the protocol ensures that the dollar value of your collateral (with haircuts and liquidation thresholds) stays ahead of the dollar value of your debt. The exact formula is in docs/LIQUIDATION.md; the intuition is: HF < 1 means “we can no longer trust this loan to be over-collateralized at current marks”, so permissionless liquidation is the standard remedy.

Cross-market aggregation means you can (unless isolated) combine multiple vaults in one user risk profile, but the registry still applies per-market parameters to each leg when computing the aggregate HF.

9. Liquidations, penalties, bad debt

Liquidations exist to protect lenders (and the protocol’s LPs / stability). A liquidator repays a slice (or, under conditions, the totality) of the borrower’s debt in return for a slice of collateral that includes a penalty / spread to compensate for execution risk. Advanced markets may have close factors (cannot unwind everything in one go unless HF is very bad), protocol fee on seized collateral, and an optional Dutch-style auction of the penalty so early liquidators get less of a bonus than late ones in stressed markets (documented in docs/LIQUIDATION.md).

Bad debt is the residual when collateral cannot cover the remaining debt. The protocol can attempt to cover from totalReserves; the uncovered remainder is recognized as a loss event. This is why reserves, caps, and conservative oracles matter: they are the shock absorbers.

10. Markets, caps, isolation

Each marketId is a bundle of: vault, oracle, risk, IRM, optional liquidation add-ons, and supply/borrow caps. Isolation mode is a firewall: some collateral types should not be cross-margined with the rest, because their correlation, oracle quality, or governance surface is different. Use isolation for long-tail or experimental collaterals.

Actual isolation rules: read MarketRegistry flags and the doc for the deployment you use.

11. ceitUSD, PSM, Router, Treasury

ceitUSD (CDP): minted when the engine issues debt, burned on repay. Minter rights are a security perimeter — production deployments usually route changes through governance and timelock, not a single EOA.

PSM addresses peg needs: 1:1-style swaps with small fees, accumulating fee reserves the DAO can move under policy. Router (if deployed) composes user intents like deposit+borrow in one go. Treasury is the protocol’s long-term value sink for fees and strategic programs.

Minter keys on CeitnotUSD and PSM are governance- or timelock-gated in a serious deployment.

12. Governance, timelock, upgradeability

CEITNOT and ve locking create long-horizon voting power; revenue may be streamed to ve holders by policy. The Governor + Timelock pattern is standard: a passed vote is queued and executed after a delay, giving the community a window to verify calldata, migrate off risky positions, and react to malicious or mistaken proposals. The engine is behind a UUPS proxy with namespaced storage (EIP-7201) to allow implementation upgrades with cleaner safety reviews than a flat storage contract.

Voters should read raw calldata in the block explorer before the delay elapses. Social consensus is a security layer, not a substitute for review.

13. Security model (overview)

On-chain money systems fail for predictable reasons. Ceitnot mitigates several classes:

  • Oracle risk — multiple feeds, staleness checks, optional TWAP / median / circuit breakers in relay variants; never trust a single DEX print for the whole market.
  • Same-block re-entrancy / flash abuse — per-position, same-block interaction guards on sensitive paths to reduce flash-loan shaped attacks.
  • Admin risk — timelock + transparent governance, production checklist for role holders.
  • Code risk — tests, audits, bug bounty. Read docs/SECURITY-AUDIT.md in the repo; this lightpaper is not a substitute for security reporting.

A diagram cannot prove safety; it only orders where to look. Verify against the specific deployment and threat model you care about.

14. What Ceitnot is not

  • Not a guarantee of positive net carry — borrow rates and price moves can dominate vault yield.
  • Not a replacement for credit assessment in the off-chain, legal, or RWA sense — it is a permissionless, over-collateralized on-chain system.
  • Not “no liquidation risk” for volatile LST / ETH — use conservative LTV and monitor HF.
  • Not an endorsement of any specific vault partner — each ERC-4626 has its own counterparty, smart contract, and depeg path.

15. FAQ

Is the frontend custodial?

No. The browser dapp only proposes transactions. Your private keys never leave the wallet. The backend, if any, is typically for config, stats, and CORS-friendly RPC — not for custody.

Does Siphon work if I do not call harvest?

Your debt will still be updated to the new scale on your next interaction (or when someone else harvests for the market). The harvest is a public good — often bots/keepers or the protocol team call it, subject to the heartbeat. If no one calls it, you still settle when you touch your position, but the public scale will not move until a successful harvest path runs.

Are borrow APR and Siphon the same “yield”?

No. Borrow APR is a cost of debt set by the IRM. Siphon is a credit from collateral revaluation and vault yield to the market’s effective debt. Think “left pocket / right pocket” in accounting terms.

Can governance brick my position?

Governance can change future parameters. Existing positions are constrained by the rules at execution time, but you should monitor proposals — this is the social layer of all DAO protocols.
